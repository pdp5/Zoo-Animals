Zoo Animals

  1. Bears

    1. Ollie
    2. Mona

    They can be as small as four feet long and about 60 pounds (the sun bear) to as big as eight feet long and more than a thousand pounds (the polar bear). They’re found throughout North America, South America, Europe, and Asia. They have nonretractable claws, short tails, and excellent sense of smell.

    1. Frankie
    2. Coconut

    The giraffe is a large African hoofed mammal belonging to the genus Giraffa. It is the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant on Earth. Traditionally, giraffes have been thought of as one species, Giraffa camelopardalis, with nine subspecies.

  3. Lions

    1. Mella
    2. Karl

    The lion, known as the king of the jungle, exudes a majestic tone of power and grace. This magnificent predator, with its imposing mane and commanding presence, roams the savannas in pride. Its regal demeanor and strength define the very essence of wildlife’s hierarchy in every paragraph written about it.

  4. Monkeys

    1. Cookie
    2. Earl
    3. Banana Pudding

    Monkeys are intelligent animals with large brains in relation to their body size. They are social animals, usually living in groups and demonstrating complex social behavior. Most monkeys are omnivores, supplementing a diet of fruit and foliage with a range of small and mid-sized animals.

  5. Alligators

    1. Wren
    2. Aspen
    3. Mika

    Large four-legged reptile with a long tail, short legs, a U-shaped snout, and a large mouth filled with conical, pointed teeth. Alligators are typically dark green to almost black in color. Their skin is tough and leathery, and there are rows of bony bumps protruding from their backs and down their tails.
